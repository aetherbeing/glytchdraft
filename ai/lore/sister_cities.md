# SISTER CITIES

The twelve real places outside Miami that hold each Order's purest form.
Loaded into Layer 2 of every agent call. Used for comparison, lore, and
analogical reasoning — the Order Chronicler and Architectural Envisioner
draw on these most. Not yet modeled in the geodata pipeline.

For each sister city: the Order it hosts, why this city specifically, and
which Miami districts it pairs with for reading-by-analogy.

---

## Las Vegas, USA — Cornucopians
The Order's home. Las Vegas does not pretend to mean anything other than
itself, which is its purest expression of spectacle. Every building advertises
itself, every advertisement is a building. Memory loops over loops. Pair with
Miami's Ocean Drive, the Collins Avenue hotel strip, and Brickell at night.

## Brasília, Brazil — Planar Witnesses
A city designed in axes by Lúcio Costa and Oscar Niemeyer; the disruption of
those axes by life is the Order's signature tension. Civic geometry that asks
to be read from above. Pair with Coral Gables (planned grid), the Civic Center
of Miami, and the Metromover's elevated geometry.

## Seoul, South Korea — Mirrorsweat
Density of glass, of screens, of surveillance, of glamour layered onto
density. Reflectivity as civic vocabulary. Pair with Brickell's glass canyons,
Aventura Mall, the Design District, and the cruise-port luxury corridor.

## Sarajevo, Bosnia — Crooked Datum
A city where the grid was contested in living memory; alignment carries
weight that elsewhere it does not. Hills against axis, monument facing the
wrong year. Pair with the diagonal of Biscayne Boulevard, the offset between
Miami's grid and Miami Beach's, the contested seawalls of the rising coast.

## Kyoto, Japan — Hollow Form
A city built around what is *not* there — the courtyard, the gravel, the
pause. Stillness as architecture. Pair with the Vizcaya gardens, the interior
voids of large Brickell towers, the unused parcels waiting between phases of
development.

## Rotterdam, Netherlands — Soft Logic
Bombed flat in 1940, rebuilt by argument. A city continuously editing itself
through public participation, retrofit, and storm response. Pair with Wynwood's
adaptive reuse, the post-Andrew rebuild patterns, the resilience corridors,
the Underline.

## Dakar, Senegal — Sash Ritual
A city of color, pattern, procession, and the textile logic of public life.
Music as urban grammar. Pair with Little Haiti, Calle Ocho, Wynwood Walls,
the carnival routes, the murals of Overtown.

## Reykjavik, Iceland — Signal Choir
Isolation that broadcasts. A city defined by weather, by the proximity of
geological forces, by the sound of broadcast carrying further when there is
less to absorb it. Pair with the National Hurricane Center in Miami, the
Virginia Key antenna farms, the cruise-port comms infrastructure, the radio
masts of the Glades.

## Leticia, Colombia — Cradle Mold
A border town on the Amazon where three countries meet and the jungle
encroaches on all of them. Threshold city. Pair with the Everglades
boundary, the mangrove parks, the Tamiami Trail edge, the canals that the
sea is taking back.

## Los Angeles, USA — The Pink Opaque
The Order's home, and a city in this project's scope (see
[[los_angeles_pink_opaque]]). Smog as cinematographer. Private myth at
metropolitan scale. Pair with Sunset Harbour, Coral Gables at golden hour,
the Fontainebleau pool deck, every west-facing terrace in Miami.

## Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia — The Cryptozoo
A city ringed by the *ger* district — a portable city that refuses to settle
fully. Strange animals in the museums, stranger animals just outside town.
Pair with Zoo Miami, the Seaquarium, the iguana suburbs, the python
corridors of the Everglades, the late-night food-truck circuits.

## Roswell, New Mexico + Pine Gap, Australia — The Blurry Uninvited
Two sites instead of one, because the Order is about *missing* singularity.
Roswell holds the consumer-friendly anomaly; Pine Gap holds the actual
restricted zone. Together they describe what cannot be seen on the map. Pair
with SOUTHCOM in Doral, Homestead Air Reserve Base, the cruise-terminal
perimeters, and any tile of LiDAR where the points stop.

## How agents use sister cities

- **Order Chronicler** — invokes the sister city when explaining why an
  Order reads the city the way it does. "Mirrorsweat looks at Brickell the
  way it looks at Gangnam — both pretending the camera isn't on."
- **Architectural Envisioner** — references sister-city precedents.
  "Rotterdam handled this with civic editing — adaptive reuse, modular
  insertion. Miami could try the same here."
- **Cinematic Director** — invokes the sister city to describe a lighting
  or color palette. "Frame this in The Pink Opaque — late LA, low sun."
- **Field Guide** — references sister cities sparingly, only when the
  user is comparing.
- **Other agents** — generally don't.

The sister cities are *analogical* anchors. They are not currently sources
of geodata in the project; do not invent buildings, parcels, or coordinates
from them.
